1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device to remember information about your visit. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to site owners.

2. Our Approach

The World Youth Initiative website is a static informational website. We have made a deliberate choice to operate without tracking cookies, behavioural analytics, or advertising technology.

This means we do not build profiles of our visitors, share browsing data with third parties, or require your consent to use basic website functionality. You can browse this website without accepting any cookie banner — because there isn't one.

3. Technical Cookies

Some cookies are technically necessary for basic website functionality. If any such cookies are set by our hosting provider (GitHub Pages) or by essential services, they are strictly required for the site to operate and are not used for tracking or personalisation purposes.

4. Embedded Content and External Links

This website does not embed third-party content such as social media widgets, YouTube videos, or advertising networks that would set cookies on your device. External links to other websites are provided for your convenience; once you leave this site, the privacy and cookie practices of those sites apply.

5. Analytics

We do not currently use any web analytics tool. If WYI chooses to introduce website analytics in the future, we are committed to using privacy-first, cookie-free analytics services (such as Plausible Analytics or Fathom Analytics) that do not require cookie consent and do not track individual users across the web.

Any such implementation will be disclosed in this Cookie Policy before it takes effect.

6. Your Control

You can manage, block, or delete any cookies through your browser settings at any time. Since we do not rely on cookies for tracking or personalisation, doing so will not affect your experience on this website.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if our practices change. The most current version will always be available on this page. We encourage you to review it periodically.

For questions about this policy, please contact us.
